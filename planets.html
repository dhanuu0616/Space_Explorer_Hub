Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury ☿

The smallest and closest planet to the Sun. It has rocky surface, no moons, and extreme temperatures. Because it has
almost no atmosphere to trap heat, it is boiling hot during the day (430°C) and freezing cold at night (-180°C).

Venus ♀️

Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system. It has thick carbon dioxide atmosphere and is often called Earth's twin due to its similar size. It is the
hottest planet in our solar system because its thick atmosphere traps heat like a greenhouse, reaching temperatures of 475°C.

Earth 🌏

The only planet known to support life, featuring abundant liquid water and a protective atmosphere. Our home and the only planet known to
support life. It is the covering about 70% of the planet, which gives it the nickname the "Blue Planet."

Mars ♂

The Red Planet known for its thin atmosphere, volcanoes, and valleys. Known as the "Red Planet" because of the iron oxide (rust) in its soil.
It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and scientists have found evidence that liquid water once flowed there.

Jupiter ♃

The largest planet in the solar system—more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined. It is
a gas giant famous for its "Great Red Spot," a giant storm that has been raging for hundreds of years.

Saturn 🪐

Famous for its beautiful ring system.Adorned with a dazzling, complex system of icy rings, Saturn is the second-largest planet.
It is a gas giant made mostly of hydrogen and helium, and it is so light that it could float in water.

Uranus ♅

An ice giant that rotates on its side and appears blue-green color caused by methane gas. Its most unusual
feature is that it rotates on its side, making it look like a rolling ball as it orbits the Sun.

Neptune ♆

The most distant planet from the Sun, it is dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds. It is a beautiful
deep blue color and takes about 165 Earth years to complete just one orbit around the Sun.
